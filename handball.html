Handball Injury Prevention & Recovery

Fractures

Causes:

  • Impact from collisions or falls
  • Being hit by the ball or equipment
  • Falls onto hard surfaces
  • Twisting injuries
  • Overuse stress fractures

Preventions:

  • Wear protective gear like padded gloves or guards
  • Ensure proper technique during play
  • Use appropriate footwear for grip and support
  • Maintain good physical conditioning
  • Avoid playing on unsafe surfaces

Recovery Methods:

  • Immobilization with casts or splints
  • Rest and avoiding stress on the injury
  • Icing to reduce swelling
  • Physiotherapy for strength rebuilding
  • Surgical intervention if necessary
Dislocations

Causes:

  • Sudden impact or collision
  • Falling awkwardly
  • Twisting or hyperextending joints
  • Forceful contact with another player
  • Overextension during jumps or dives

Preventions:

  • Strengthen muscles around joints
  • Use protective gear
  • Practice proper falling techniques
  • Warm-up thoroughly before games
  • Maintain good flexibility

Recovery Methods:

  • Immediate immobilization and medical reduction
  • Rest and ice application
  • Pain management
  • Physiotherapy for joint stability
  • Gradual return to activity with supervised exercises
Strains

Causes:

  • Sudden acceleration or deceleration
  • Overstretching or overexertion
  • Poor conditioning
  • Inadequate warm-up
  • Repetitive stress

Preventions:

  • Warm-up and stretch
  • Strengthen muscles
  • Maintain flexibility
  • Progress gradually in training
  • Rest adequately between sessions

Recovery Methods:

  • Rest and ice
  • Compression and elevation
  • Physiotherapy
  • Gradual stretching and strengthening
  • Use of support braces if needed
Sprains

Causes:

  • Twisting or rolling joints
  • Impact during collisions
  • Landing awkwardly
  • SUDDEN movements
  • Playing on uneven surfaces

Preventions:

  • Strengthen surrounding muscles
  • Use supportive gear
  • Proper warm-up and stretching
  • Avoid sudden untrained motions
  • Use taping or braces if prone

Recovery Methods:

  • Rest and immobilize
  • Ice the area
  • Compression bandages
  • Elevation
  • Physiotherapy

About Sport

Handball is a fast-paced team sport where two teams of seven players each aim to score goals by throwing a ball into the opposing team’s net. Played mainly indoors, players use their hands to pass, dribble, and shoot the ball, while trying to outmaneuver defenders. The game emphasizes speed, agility, and teamwork, and is popular worldwide, especially in Europe.
